# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We pledge to make participation in the SGRX community welcoming, safe, and
equitable for everyone. We will respect the dignity, rights, experiences, and
contributions of all people who participate in good faith.

## Expected behavior

Community members are expected to:

- Engage kindly, honestly, and constructively.
- Respect different viewpoints and experiences.
- Give and receive feedback with empathy.
- Take responsibility for mistakes and work to repair harm.
- Respect the purpose of the project and the safety of its community.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following behaviors are not acceptable:

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or unwanted personal attention.
- Insults, personal attacks, discriminatory remarks, or demeaning comments.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or attention that is inappropriate to the
  project community.
- Publishing or acting on another person's private information without
  permission.
- Impersonation, deliberate disruption, or evasion of moderation actions.
- Other conduct that could reasonably threaten the safety or well-being of the
  community.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all SGRX project spaces. It also applies when a
person is officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Reporting

Report possible violations privately through the repository's GitHub Security
Advisories page by selecting **Report a vulnerability** and prefixing the report
title with `[Conduct]`. Do not open a public issue or disclose private details in
public.

Repository maintainers will review reports confidentially, investigate fairly,
and take action appropriate to the circumstances. Anyone responsible for
handling a report must disclose conflicts of interest and step aside when
necessary.

## Enforcement

Depending on the impact and severity of a violation, maintainers may issue a
private warning, limit participation temporarily, suspend participation, or
permanently remove a person from project spaces. Enforcement decisions will
prioritize community safety, confidentiality, proportionality, and the
opportunity to repair harm where appropriate.
